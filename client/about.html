Our Mission

Developing the next generation of Kent chess.

The Kent Junior Chess Association (KJCA) is a non-profit organization dedicated to fostering a love for chess among young players across Kent and South-East London.

Who We Are

For decades, the KJCA has served as the heartbeat of junior chess in the county. We aren't just a tournament organizer; we are a community-led association responsible for the development of junior chess from the first tentative moves in a primary school club to international-level competition.

We operate in close affiliation with the Kent County Chess Association (KCCA) and the English Chess Federation (ECF), ensuring that every child in Kent has a clear pathway to improve their game and represent their county at a national level.

What We Do

The Grand Prix Series

Our flagship annual competition. A series of tournaments held across the county where players earn points throughout the season to be crowned KJCA Champions.

World-Class Coaching

We run dedicated training days featuring some of the best junior coaches in the country, tailored to all levels from absolute beginners to advanced experts.

County Representation

We select and manage the Kent junior teams that compete in the EPSCA and ECF national championships, continuing Kent's proud history of success.

ECF Rating

All our tournament games are officially rated by the English Chess Federation, allowing juniors to track their progress and see their national standing improve.

A Safe Place to Play

"The KJCA is committed to providing a safe, inclusive, and encouraging environment. We adopt full ECF Safeguarding policies and work closely with venues like Sevenoaks School and Chislehurst School for Girls to ensure our events meet the highest standards for our young players and their families."
